## Questions to Ask Pattern First
1. How long does a CSM currently spend on needs assessment from handoff to first plan delivery? (baseline for time-saved)
2. What does "successfully onboarded" mean at Pattern? Is there a formal milestone or health score threshold?
3. What tools does the CS team use? (Gainsight, Totango, Salesforce, Google Docs, Notion?)
4. What's the average time from handoff to customer first-value milestone?
5. What's the churn rate in the first 90 days vs. after?
6. How many customers does a typical CSM manage? What's capacity headroom today?
7. What are the most common reasons an onboarding goes poorly?
8. What's the current first-plan quality like? How often does the plan change significantly after kickoff?

## Key Metrics

### Efficiency
- **Time-to-plan:** Hours from data ingestion to first roadmap delivery (Patrick vs. baseline)
- **CSM review time:** How long a CSM spends validating/editing Patrick's output (target: <30 min)
- **Time-to-value:** Days from handoff to customer first meaningful outcome

### Quality
- **Needs accuracy rate:** % of Patrick-identified needs validated by CSM (target: >80% after seeding, >90% after 6 months of flywheel)
- **False negative rate:** % of CSM-added needs Patrick missed (target: <15%)
- **Roadmap adoption rate:** % of Phase 1 items completed within their target window
- **Plan revision frequency:** How often does the roadmap change significantly post-delivery

### Business Impact
- Customer health score at 30/60/90 days post-onboarding (Patrick-assisted vs. pre-Patrick baseline)
- NRR correlation: Do customers with high roadmap adoption have better net revenue retention?
- Churn rate in first 90 days (should decline as onboarding quality improves)
- CSM capacity: Customers per CSM over time

## Stakeholder Communication
- **Monthly "Patrick Report":** 1-page digest — time saved (hours × rate), needs accuracy trend, roadmap completion %, customers onboarded with vs. without Patrick
- **Quarterly deep-dive:** Customer health score comparison, churn delta, CSM capacity headroom created
- **Framing:** "Patrick did in 4 minutes what used to take 3 hours" beats abstract percentages — use per-customer narratives alongside aggregates
- **Outlier cases:** When Patrick catches a need the CSM hadn't surfaced, document it. Anecdotes build stakeholder trust faster than dashboards.

## Time Saved Math (example)
Pre-Patrick: 3 hrs/customer × 5 new customers/month/CSM = 15 hrs/month on analysis per CSM
Post-Patrick: 30 min/customer × 5 = 2.5 hrs/month
**= 12.5 hrs/month freed per CSM**
At 10 CSMs = 125 hrs/month = ~3 FTE-equivalent capacity without hiring

For enterprise customers (6–8 hrs baseline), savings are proportionally larger — and quality is higher because Patrick doesn't miss things at 11pm before a kickoff call.
