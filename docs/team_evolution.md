## The Right Framing
Not "Patrick does your job" — but "Patrick does the part of your job that doesn't require you."

Pattern's CSMs have deep customer intuition, relationship skills, and product expertise that no AI has. What they've been spending significant time on — reading through transcripts, scoring needs, formatting plans — is valuable work done by people who are overqualified for it. Patrick takes the analytical scaffolding so CSMs spend more time on the work only they can do.

## CSM Role Shift
- **Today:** Analyst + relationship manager + project manager
- **With Patrick:** Strategic advisor + relationship manager + innovation driver
- **Patrick handles:** Data synthesis, needs scoring, plan structuring, progress reporting
- **CSM handles:** Relationship, judgment calls, organizational navigation, strategic guidance, feedback to Patrick

## What Freed Capacity Enables
1. More customers per CSM (capacity expansion without headcount)
2. Deeper engagement with strategic accounts (more call time, less prep time)
3. Proactive outreach — getting ahead of problems instead of reacting
4. Product feedback loops — synthesizing customer insights for the product team
5. Playbook development — building onboarding playbooks for specific segments
6. Customer community and education — content, webinars, peer connections
7. Expansion selling — Patrick's identified needs are expansion conversation hooks

## Capacity Math
Pre-Patrick: 3 hrs/customer, 5 new/month/CSM = 15 hrs/month on analysis
Post-Patrick: 30 min/customer = 2.5 hrs/month
→ 12.5 hrs/month freed per CSM → 125 hrs/month across 10 CSMs → ~3 FTE equivalent

For enterprise customers the savings are proportionally larger (6–8 hr baseline vs 3 hr SMB baseline).

## How to Measure Team Impact
- Hours-per-onboarding before/after Patrick (time tracking or CSM self-report)
- Customers-per-CSM over time
- Customer CSAT on kickoff call quality (did the CSM seem prepared?)
- CSM internal NPS (are they doing more meaningful work?)
- Proactive outreach rate (did CSMs reach out before problems, or after?)

## Change Management
**Critical:** Position CSMs as Patrick's trainers, not Patrick's replacements. Their feedback makes Patrick better — they own the quality of the system. This framing drives adoption and makes CSMs invested in Patrick's success rather than threatened by it.

The CSMs who engage most with the feedback interface will see Patrick improve fastest for their customers. Make that visible and reward it.
